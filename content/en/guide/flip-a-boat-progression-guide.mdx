<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Restoration: Rebuilding the Engine">

Rebuilding the engine requires a systematic approach. Removing parts in the wrong order can make it difficult to access deeper components, such as the fuel filter or spark plugs. Follow this structured assembly line to minimize build times.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Dismantle the Rusted Engine">
    Interact with the computer, select edit mode, and manually detach the rusted propeller, water pump, carburetor, air box, fuel filter, and spark plugs. Place these old components in the designated discard area on your plot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Install the New Propeller and Pump">
    Pick up the new propeller, align it with the rear drive shaft, and press the interaction key (default: F) to snap it into place. Repeat this process for the upgraded water pump.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Mount the Carburetor and Air Box">
    Attach the new carburetor directly to the engine intake manifold. Once secured, mount the air box over the carburetor intake to complete the primary air delivery system.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure the Fuel System and Spark Plugs">
    Fit the small fuel filter into its slot near the fuel line. Finally, insert the new spark plugs into the engine head to ensure the ignition system is fully operational.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once the mechanical components are secured, verify that all connections are highlighted in green, indicating a successful installation. A single missing spark plug or misaligned filter can prevent the boat from being recognized as complete.

| Assembly Step | Key Component | Tool/Keybind | Difficulty Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| Step 1 | Engine Block | Grab / Drag | Easy |
| Step 2 | Water Pump | F Key (Snap) | Easy |
| Step 3 | Carburetor | F Key (Snap) | Medium |
| Step 4 | Spark Plugs | F Key (Snap) | Easy |
