The progression in Flip A Boat is structured around a buy-repair-sell cycle. Unlike simple clicker games, this simulator requires physical transport of your goods and manual inspection of parts. Success is measured by your "Profit Margin"—the difference between the final sale price and the sum of the purchase price and part costs.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select a Manageable Project">
    Visit the boat listings and look for a hull that fits your current budget while leaving at least 30% for parts. Beginners should always start with the **11-Foot Starter Project**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Haul">
    Drive your truck and trailer to the boat's location. Back the trailer up slowly until the hitch prompt appears. Once attached, align the trailer with the boat and use the "Secure" interaction before driving to your workshop.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Component Inspection">
    Inside the workshop, interact with the boat to highlight damaged areas. Check the hull for cracks, the stern for engine issues, and the helm for broken gauges or steering wheels.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Systematic Repair">
    Purchase parts through the repair interface. Prioritize mechanical systems (Outboards and Steering) first. A boat that looks pretty but doesn't run will not fetch its maximum value.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Final Sale">
    Once all required repairs are 100% complete, transport the boat to the launch ramp. Test it on the water if desired, then finalize the sale to reinvest your profits into a larger vessel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Truck} title="Trucks, Trailers, and Hauling Physics">

Hauling is the "hidden boss" of this simulator. Many players lose time and money by flipping their trailers or losing boats on the highway. Using the right equipment for the job is essential as you progress to larger 40-foot hulls.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="The Perfect Alignment">
    Always approach the trailer hitch in a straight line. Use the **first-person camera** or the top-down view to ensure the truck's ball hitch is perfectly centered with the trailer tongue. Small steering inputs are better than large corrections.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Securing the Load">
    Once the boat is on the trailer, you must use the interaction prompt to "Secure Boat." Failing to do this will cause the boat to slide off during acceleration or when climbing the steep launch ramps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Braking and Weight">
    Heavier boats increase your stopping distance. When hauling a triple or quad-engine vessel, begin braking much earlier than you would with an empty trailer. Avoid "slamming" the brakes to prevent jackknifing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Launch Ramp Safety">
    When reversing down the ramp, keep your wheels straight. Only submerge the trailer deep enough for the boat to float. If you drive the truck too deep, you risk flooding the engine and incurring extra costs.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Physics Glitches">
If your boat becomes stuck in a wall or the trailer starts vibrating violently, detach the hitch immediately. This usually resets the physics constraints and prevents your vehicle from launching into the sky.
</Callout>

</Section>
