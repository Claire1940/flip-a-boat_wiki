<Section icon={Shield} title="The Restoration Process: Step-by-Step">

Once you have secured your parts and returned to your plot, the real work begins. You must use your plot's computer to enter the **Mechanic Mode**. This interface allows you to see the boat's internal structure and identify which parts are rusted and which are functional. Restoration is a two-part process: stripping the old and installing the new.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Teardown">
    Access the computer, go to **Manage**, and select **Edit Boat**. Enter the **Mechanic** tab. Remove every single rusted part (they appear brown/orange). Drag them to the side of your plot to clear the workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Component Installation">
    Pick up your new parts from the inventory. Align them with the highlighted ghost-frames on the engine block. Press the **F key** to snap the part into place. Ensure the water pump and propeller are connected correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Engine Calibration">
    Install the spark plugs, carburetor, and fuel filter. These parts are small and can be tricky to click. Zoom in your camera for better precision during this phase.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Assembly">
    Attach the oil tank and the air box. Once all parts are installed, the engine should no longer show any "Missing Part" warnings in the mechanic interface.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Efficiency Gain">
Tearing down the entire boat before starting the installation phase is much faster than replacing parts one by one. It prevents you from accidentally clicking on a new part while trying to remove an old one.
</Callout>

| Action | Keybind / Tool | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Remove Part** | Click + Drag | Clears rusted components |
| **Snap Part** | **F Key** | Securely attaches new part |
| **Rotate Camera** | Right Click | Better view of small parts |
| **Zoom In** | Mouse Wheel | Precise placement of plugs |

</Section>
