<Section icon={Target} title="The Mechanical Restoration Process">

Once you have secured your parts and returned to your plot, the real work begins. The **flip a boat beginner guide** workflow is divided into two phases: Deconstruction and Assembly. You cannot install new parts until every piece of "rust" has been removed from the existing frame. This requires precision and a systematic approach to ensure no small components are left behind.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access the Boat Management System">
    Head to the computer on your plot. Select **Manage**, then **Edit Boat**, and finally enter the **Mechanic** interface. This opens the 3D view of your vessel's internals.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Strip the Rusted Components">
    Manually click and drag every rusted part off the boat. This includes the old propeller, the water pump, the carburetor, and the spark plugs. Move these to the side of your workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Install New Hardware">
    Pick up your new parts from the inventory. Align them with the highlighted ghost-frames on the engine and press the **F key** to snap them into place. Ensure the propeller and water pump are seated correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Component Check">
    Verify that the oil tank, fuel filter, and air box are all installed. The engine should look clean and free of any brown, rusted textures before you move to the next stage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Action | Key Bind | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pick Up Part** | Left Click | Holds the item in your hand |
| **Snap Part** | F Key | Automatically locks part to the boat |
| **Rotate View** | Right Click + Drag | Changes your perspective of the engine |
| **Open Menu** | E Key | Interacts with the Plot Computer |

<Callout type="tip" title="Efficiency Tip">
Tear the entire boat apart first before picking up a single new part. Keeping your "old" and "new" piles separated prevents confusion and ensures you don't accidentally reinstall a rusted component.
</Callout>
