<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Restoration Process">

To maximize your returns, you must follow a structured restoration workflow. Deviating from this order can lead to wasted materials and redundant labor.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquisition & Transport">
    Purchase the vessel from the salvage yard or auction house. Secure it on your transport trailer and haul it to the main workshop bay.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Hull Stripping & Cleaning">
    Use the high-pressure washer to remove marine growth, barnacles, and peeling paint. Sand the hull down to the base material to prepare for patch work.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Structural Repairs">
    Patch any fiberglass cracks or replace rotted wooden planks. Ensure the hull is completely watertight before proceeding to cosmetic upgrades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Mechanical Overhaul">
    Rebuild or replace the engine, service the steering cables, and update the electrical wiring. A certified functional engine is required for top-tier valuation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Cosmetic Detailing & Sale">
    Apply marine-grade paint, polish all chrome fittings, and install fresh upholstery. List the boat on the market and negotiate with interested buyers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Never skip the structural repair step. Selling a boat with hidden hull damage can lead to severe reputation penalties and refund demands from buyers.
</Callout>

</Section>
