<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Restoration Tutorial">

To ensure your build qualifies as a **flip a boat fastest boat**, follow this precise mechanical sequence. Improperly ordered assembly can lead to "ghost parts" or clipping issues that prevent the boat from being sold or operated at maximum efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Strip the Vessel">
    Access the boat through the computer terminal under "Manage" > "Edit Boat" > "Mechanic." Remove every single rusty part, including the propeller, water pump, spark plugs, and fuel filters. Move them to a designated scrap area on your plot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Core Engine Installation">
    Install the new engine block first. Use the **F key** to snap the motor onto the mounting brackets. Ensure the alignment is perfect before proceeding to smaller attachments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Ancillary Systems">
    Attach the water pump and the propeller to the rear of the engine. Then, move to the top of the block to install the three spark plugs, the oil tank, and the fuel filter. The carburetor should be one of the final mechanical pieces added.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Calibration">
    Double-check all connections. A single missing spark plug or a loose fuel filter will prevent the boat from reaching its maximum rated speed, significantly lowering the "Flip" profit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Mechanism Tip">
The "Snap" feature (F key) is context-sensitive. If a part won't snap, you may have another component blocking the slot. Always install internal parts before external casings.
</Callout>
