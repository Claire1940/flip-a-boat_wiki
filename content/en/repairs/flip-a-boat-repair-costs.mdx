<Section icon={Target} title="Mechanical and Engine Restoration Expenses">

The propulsion system is the heart of the vessel and usually the most expensive component in the **flip a boat repair costs** spreadsheet. Whether you are dealing with an outboard, inboard, or sterndrive (I/O), the goal is to achieve mechanical reliability without over-capitalizing on a boat with a low ceiling price.

In 2026, 4-stroke outboard engines are highly sought after in the used market. If your project boat comes with an older 2-stroke, consider if the cost of a "re-power" (replacing the engine) is justifiable. Often, a non-running engine only requires a carburetor cleaning, new spark plugs, and a fresh fuel pump to regain its value.

| Engine Component | Service/Repair Cost | Replacement Cost | Priority Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fuel System** | $200 - $500 | $1,200 - $2,000 | High |
| **Cooling Impeller** | $50 - $150 | $300 - $500 | Critical |
| **Lower Unit/Drive** | $400 - $800 | $2,500 - $4,500 | High |
| **Propeller** | $100 - $250 | $300 - $700 | Moderate |
| **Control Cables** | $150 - $300 | $500 - $900 | Moderate |

<Callout type="tip" title="Mechanical Strategy">
Always perform a compression test before buying a project boat. A healthy engine should have cylinders within 10% of each other. Low compression in one cylinder usually indicates a major rebuild is needed, which rarely makes sense for a flip.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Drain Old Fuel">
    Stale gasoline is the #1 cause of engine failure. Completely drain the tank and inspect for debris or water.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Ignition System Check">
    Replace spark plugs and check for a strong spark. Inspect the wiring harness for corrosion or rodent damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Cooling System Service">
    Replace the water pump impeller regardless of its appearance. Overheating can destroy an engine in minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Gear Lube Change">
    Drain the lower unit oil. If it looks milky, the seals are leaking and must be replaced to prevent gear failure.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Interior and Cosmetic Refurbishment">

Cosmetics are what sell the boat. While mechanical reliability is essential, the visual appeal is what allows you to command a premium price. Modernizing the interior is a high-impact way to manage **flip a boat repair costs** while seeing a direct increase in market value.

Marine-grade vinyl is expensive, but it is one area where you should not cut corners. Cheap automotive vinyl will crack under UV exposure within a single season. Similarly, upgrading the flooring from old marine carpet to modern EVA foam decking (like SeaDek) is a massive selling point in 2026.

<Section icon={Map} title="Hidden Costs: Trailers and Registration">

One of the most overlooked aspects of **flip a boat repair costs** is the trailer. A boat is significantly harder to sell without a road-worthy trailer. Tires, bearings, and lighting are the most common points of failure that flippers forget to budget for.

Furthermore, administrative costs can eat into your margins. Ensure the boat has a clear title or hull identification number (HIN) that matches the registration. Buying a "title-less" boat is a major risk that can lead to a total loss of investment if you cannot legally transfer ownership to the buyer.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Wheel Bearings** | $40 - $100 | Moderate | Critical |
| **LED Light Kit** | $50 - $120 | Easy | High |
| **Winch Strap** | $20 - $50 | Easy | Moderate |
