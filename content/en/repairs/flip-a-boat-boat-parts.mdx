<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Boat Repair Guide">

Restoring a boat requires a systematic approach to ensure no parts are missed. The game's interface highlights damaged areas, but knowing the order of operations can save you time and prevent wasted movement around the workshop.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Workshop Positioning">
    Haul your project boat into the designated repair area using your truck. Ensure the trailer is straight and the boat is fully within the interaction zone. If the boat is not correctly positioned, the repair prompts for specific **flip a boat boat parts** may not appear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mechanical Inspection">
    Walk around the vessel and interact with the highlighted points. The game will identify exactly which parts are missing or broken. This typically includes the outboard motor components, the steering wheel at the helm, and various dashboard gauges.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Part Acquisition">
    Access the in-game parts store. You must purchase the specific components identified during your inspection. Be careful to match the part tier with your boat size; installing high-end parts on a starter boat can erase your profit margin.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Installation & Assembly">
    Return to the boat and interact with the damaged slots to install your new parts. Start with the engine at the stern, then move to the center console for steering and electronics. Finally, address any structural hull repairs required by the interface.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Pre-Sale Validation">
    Once all required repairs are complete, the boat is technically ready for sale. Perform a final walkthrough to ensure no highlighted points remain. At this stage, you can choose to apply paint, though this is purely cosmetic and should only be done if you have a significant budget surplus.
  </Step>
</Steps>
