<Section icon={Shield} title="Structural and Hull Upgrades">

The structural integrity of your vessel determines its baseline safety rating and appeal to high-end buyers. A cracked transom or soft deck will severely limit your sale price, regardless of how powerful the engine is. When working on hull restorations, prioritize sealing leaks and replacing rotted core materials. 

Fiberglass repairs require patience and the correct application of gel coats. For aluminum hulls, focus on reinforcing rivets and welding structural seams. The table below outlines the primary hull upgrades available in the workshop.

| Upgrade Type | Base Material Cost | Time to Install | Value Multiplier |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fiberglass Patch** | 350 Credits | 2 Hours | 1.15x |
| **Reinforced Transom** | 850 Credits | 5 Hours | 1.30x |
| **Aluminum Weld Seal** | 600 Credits | 3 Hours | 1.20x |
| **Gel Coat Refinishing** | 1,200 Credits | 8 Hours | 1.45x |
| **Carbon Core Decking** | 2,500 Credits | 12 Hours | 1.80x |

Investing in premium decking materials like synthetic teak improves aesthetics while offering excellent durability. This upgrade is highly recommended for luxury class cruisers and sport fishing vessels.

<Callout type="warning" title="Structural Integrity Warning">
Do not apply premium gel coats over unpatched structural cracks. The cosmetic finish will quickly deteriorate, leading to failed inspections and reduced buyer trust.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Upgrade Process">

Executing your restoration project in the correct order saves time and minimizes structural rework. Follow this standardized workshop sequence to optimize your workflow.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stripping and Cleaning">
    Remove all rotted seats, broken electronics, and corroded wiring. Pressure wash the interior and exterior to reveal hidden structural damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Structural Reinforcement">
    Patch the hull, reinforce the transom, and replace soft deck sections. Ensure the vessel is completely watertight before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Electrical Rewiring">
    Install a new wiring harness, upgrade the breaker panels, and mount modern bilge pumps. Check all connections for proper grounding.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Propulsion Installation">
    Mount the engine, align the drive shafts, and connect steering cables. Perform a dry-dock system test to verify throttle response.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Cosmetics and Detailing">
    Apply marine-grade paint, install new upholstery, and mount navigation displays. Polish all metal fixtures to maximize visual appeal.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Efficiency Boost">
Completing all structural repairs before purchasing electrical components prevents parts from getting damaged by fiberglass dust or welding sparks.
</Callout>

</Section>
