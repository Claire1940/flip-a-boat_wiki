Restoring a vessel requires a meticulous, part-by-part approach. The game does not allow you to skip straight to painting or selling; mechanical and structural integrity must be restored first. The repair interface highlights damaged components when you interact with the boat in your designated workshop area.

Follow this structured workflow to ensure no component is overlooked during the restoration process.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure and Position the Vessel">
    Haul your purchased boat to the workshop using your truck and trailer. Park the trailer completely within the yellow boundary lines of your repair bay. Confirm that the repair interaction prompt appears on your screen before detaching the tow vehicle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Conduct a Complete Damage Inspection">
    Walk around the hull and climb onto the deck. Interact with the diagnostic menu to highlight all damaged parts. Common issues include cracked fiberglass, blown outboard motors, broken steering wheels, and shattered helm gauges.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Mechanical and Engine Repairs">
    Prioritize the propulsion and steering systems. Purchase the exact replacement parts required from the in-game store. For larger hulls, you will need to replace multiple outboard engines at the stern, ensuring all electrical connections are restored.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Restore the Helm and Electronics">
    Replace damaged steering wheels, analog gauges, and digital navigation screens at the center console. These components are essential for restoring the vessel's operational rating, which directly influences its final market value.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Apply Cosmetic Customization and Polish">
    Once all structural and mechanical repairs are at 100%, access the customization booth. Apply fresh paint schemes and hull decals. Keep cosmetic spending low on resale projects, but feel free to customize boats destined for your permanent collection.
  </Step>
